BLACKBERRY-PICKING







“Late August, given heavy rain and sun
For a full week, the blackberries would ripen.
At first, just one, a glossy purple clot
Among others, red, green, hard as a knot.
You ate that first one and its flesh was sweet
….
We hoarded the fresh berries in the byre.
But when the bath was filled we found a fur,
A rat-grey fungus, glutting on our cache.
The juice was stinking too. Once off the bush
The fruit fermented, the sweet flesh would turn sour.
I always felt like crying. It wasn't fair
That all the lovely canfuls smelt of rot.
Each year I hoped they'd keep, knew they would not.”


– Blackberry-Picking by Seamus Heaney
The setting of this project is actually modeled after my own apartment in New York. The room adjacent to the one displayed as the background is mine. The reason I chose “Blackberry-Picking” to represent my room (which has since been colonized by my sister after my departure) is because of this poem’s commentary on childhood and adulthood. I share the speaker’s sentiments on childhood memories, wishing I could somehow relive the time I spent with the people I cherish most. I haven’t been an “adult” for long (nor do I feel like one in any sense) but the word already leaves a sour taste in my mouth. I hope that someday I will appreciate the taste, as fruit ferments to wine.
